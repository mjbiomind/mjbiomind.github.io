Couture · About

Mary Jacobs

Mary Jacobs is a bilingual biostatistics and health data science professional based in San Francisco. Her work connects clinical research, statistical reasoning, and computational methods to study trauma, psychiatric outcomes, and health disparities.

She has supported research across psychiatry, neurology, and large-scale educational assessment at UCSF, with experience in REDCap, Epic/APeX, OnCore, clinical research operations, data quality, and reproducible analysis. Her developing research program centers on causal inference, clinical NLP, survival analysis, and predictive modeling for mental health equity, particularly among Spanish-speaking and historically underrepresented communities.

Her long-term objective is to conduct doctoral-level biomedical data science research that translates complex clinical data into precise, ethical, and socially meaningful decisions.

Fine Jewelry · Research

Research Direction

How can statistical learning identify modifiable pathways between trauma, physiology, social conditions, and long-term mental health outcomes?

Current interests include causal inference for observational health data, interpretable machine learning, clinical NLP, survival analysis, computational psychiatry, and responsible AI. The central standard is methodological rigor paired with direct relevance to patients and communities.
